Our story

Built by an instructor who got tired of paying $100/month to earn $0.

Where this started

A few years ago, we needed to teach a small group of clients a skills-based process. Simple enough ‚Äî except the platform options were either clunky and old, or slick and expensive.

We signed up for one of the popular platforms. $119/month. We built our course, enrolled our first five learners, and held our breath. Two months later, we had made $0 in revenue from the course ‚Äî but we'd paid $238 to host it.

"The platform assumed we were already profitable. We were just getting started."

That's when the frustration turned into a question: why does teaching online have to cost this much before you've even proven there's demand?

We looked at the features we actually used: a course builder, lesson pages, progress tracking, and a way to collect payment. That's it. Everything else ‚Äî live webinar tools, advanced funnels, complex affiliate systems ‚Äî we didn't touch. But we were paying for all of it.

What we built instead

LearnAtYourOwnPace is the platform we wished existed. It's designed for instructors who want to start lean, test their content with real learners, and grow without being taxed into unprofitability by their own tools.

We made one deliberate trade-off: we don't host video. You link your videos from wherever you already have them ‚Äî YouTube unlisted, Vimeo, Loom, wherever. This keeps our infrastructure costs low, which lets us keep the price low. It also keeps you in control of your content.

Everything else ‚Äî course builder, quizzes, completion certificates, progress analytics, payments ‚Äî is built in. Not added as a premium upsell. Just there, for $20/month.

Who this is for

Independent instructors. Coaches. Corporate trainers building internal learning materials. Subject matter experts who've been told "you should teach a course" but don't want to over-invest before proving the concept.

We're not built for the VC-funded EdTech company with a team of engineers. We're built for the person who knows their subject, wants to teach it well, and wants a platform that gets out of the way.
